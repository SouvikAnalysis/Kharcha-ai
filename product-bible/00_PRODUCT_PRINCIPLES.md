# Purpose

This document defines the core principles that guide every product decision made for Kharcha.

These principles act as the foundation of the product.

Whenever a new feature, design, or technical decision is proposed, it must be evaluated against these principles.

If a decision violates these principles, it should be reconsidered.

---

# Product Philosophy

Kharcha is not just an expense tracker.

Kharcha is a personal finance companion that helps people understand their money with the least possible effort.

We believe that financial awareness should be simple, fast, and accessible to everyone.

---

# Our Mission

Help people build better financial habits without making money management feel like accounting.

---

# Product Principles

## 1. Every Rupee Matters

Every expense tells a story.

No expense is too small to understand.

Every feature should help users become more aware of how they spend their money.

---

## 2. Busy People Come First

Our users have jobs, studies, families, and responsibilities.

Kharcha should respect their time.

The app should never feel like extra work.

---

## 3. Less Typing, More Tapping

Typing slows users down.

The interface should minimize manual input wherever possible.

Examples:

- Frequently used categories
- One-tap expense entry
- Smart defaults
- Quick actions

---

## 4. Five-Second Rule

A user should be able to record an expense in less than five seconds.

If a feature increases the time required to record an expense, it should be redesigned.

---

## 5. Show Insights, Not Just Numbers

Numbers alone do not change behavior.

Kharcha should explain spending patterns in a way users can easily understand.

Instead of only showing charts, the app should provide meaningful observations and actionable insights.

---

## 6. Simplicity Is a Feature

Every additional button, field, and option increases complexity.

If something can be simplified, it should be.

A clean interface is one of Kharcha's biggest strengths.

---

## 7. Mobile-First Experience

Kharcha is designed primarily for smartphones.

Every interaction should be optimized for one-handed use and quick access.

---

## 8. India First

Kharcha is built with Indian users in mind.

Examples include:

- Indian currency (₹)
- Indian spending habits
- Salary cycles
- Local budgeting behavior
- Common expense categories

Future decisions should continue to prioritize Indian users.

---

## 9. Privacy by Default

Financial data is personal.

Users own their data.

Kharcha should collect only the information necessary to deliver its features.

Privacy and security should never be compromised.

---

## 10. Build Habits, Not Dependency

The goal is not to make users spend more time inside the app.

The goal is to help them build sustainable financial habits.

If users become more financially aware while spending less time managing money, Kharcha has succeeded.

---

# Decision Filter

Before adding any feature, ask the following questions:

- Does this help users understand their money?
- Does this help users control their spending?
- Does it save users time?
- Is it simple to use?
- Does it improve the overall experience?
- Is it aligned with Kharcha's mission?

If the answer to most of these questions is **No**, the feature should not be included.

---

# What We Believe

We believe people do not need another accounting application.

They need clarity.

They need awareness.

They need confidence in their financial decisions.

Kharcha exists to provide that confidence through thoughtful design and meaningful insights.

---

# Product Promise

Every screen.

Every feature.

Every interaction.

Should help users answer one simple question:

> **"Where did my money go?"**

And eventually replace it with:

> **"I know exactly where every rupee went."**

---

# Guiding Principle

When faced with multiple solutions, always choose the one that:

- Requires the fewest steps
- Is easiest to understand
- Saves the most time
- Provides the greatest value to the user

Simplicity is not the absence of features.

Simplicity is the presence of clarity.

---

# Final Principle

If a feature does not help users understand or control their money more easily, it does not belong in Kharcha.

Everything we build must earn its place.
